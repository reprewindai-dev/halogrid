The Command Center

5 Zones

The interface is divided into a top bar, left intelligence rail, center routing matrix, right efficiency stack, and bottom telemetry bar. Each zone exists to answer a different operator question: What is happening, where is it happening, how clean is it, and do I need to intervene?

TOP BAR → system status + score + controls LEFT PANEL → route nodes + live alerts CENTER CANVAS → global routing matrix RIGHT PANEL → trend + efficiency gauges BOTTOM BAR → live telemetry strip

Top Bar

Vitals

The top bar is your fastest health check. It contains the system status, the live CO2 Score pill, and controls.

ElementMeaning
System StatusNOMINAL means routes are healthy; DEGRADED means the system is compensating; CRITICAL means operator attention is recommended.
CO2 Score PillThe live 0–100 carbon efficiency reading for the active route.
Pulse EffectA visual heartbeat confirming fresh data has arrived.

Left Panel

Route Intelligence

This panel is the route roster and alert feed. Every row represents a region the system can evaluate or use. The highlighted route is the one currently active.

Each route row shows a region ID, current route score, and grid intensity in gCO₂/kWh. Lower intensity usually means a cleaner path.

Center Canvas

Routing Matrix

The center canvas is the visual identity of the product. Glowing nodes represent regions, and dashed links represent candidate pathways. It is less a map than a routing theater.

  • Teal nodes indicate strong carbon performance
  • Amber nodes indicate moderate performance
  • Red nodes indicate high-emission regions that should usually be avoided

Right Panel

Trend & Efficiency

The right stack answers two questions: how the system has been performing, and which global zones are strongest right now. The chart tells the story over time; the gauges tell the story at a glance.

Bottom Bar

Live Telemetry

The lower strip is a compact KPI deck. It tracks latency, saved emissions, reroutes, uptime, and warnings. It is meant for constant peripheral awareness rather than deep reading.

Understanding the CO2 Score

Core Metric

The CO2 Score is the product's headline metric. It translates regional carbon intensity into a single number that is easy to understand without hiding the seriousness underneath.

ScoreInterpretation
85–100Excellent — the route is among the cleanest currently available
70–84Good — the system is in a healthy range with room for improvement
50–69Fair — cleaner alternatives may exist or be emerging
Below 50Poor — operator review or automatic reroute is expected

Alert Levels

Needs Attention

Alerts appear in natural language so operators do not need to decode raw logs. Most alert types are informational. Critical alerts should be reviewed.

Grid Intensity

gCO₂/kWh

Grid intensity measures how much carbon is emitted to generate a unit of electricity in a region. Lower numbers mean cleaner electricity and cleaner routing decisions.

RangeRatingTypical Mix
0–100ExcellentHydro, wind, solar dominant
101–200GoodMixed grid with moderate renewables
201–350FairHeavier fossil fuel usage
351+PoorCarbon-heavy grid conditions

FAQ

Operator Notes

Does CO2 Router slow traffic down?
Not materially. Route evaluation happens before dispatch and is designed to be lightweight.

Can operators force a route?
Yes. A node can be selected manually, after which the UI indicates override state until auto mode resumes.

Why does the score move even if nothing changed?
Because regional grids are alive. Renewable output and energy demand change constantly.
